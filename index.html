Project Overview

URBAN-AI leverages cutting-edge artificial intelligence tools such as GPT-4V and Grounding DINO to create a comprehensive global resource cadastre. Our goal is to advance circular economy practices by addressing the challenge of insufficient building data, enabling sustainable and efficient urban development.

By integrating AI-powered analysis of building facades using street-level imagery, URBAN-AI offers insights into material inventorying, resource allocation, and urban planning strategies, specifically tailored for diverse urban environments around the world.

Methodology

URBAN-AI utilizes a workflow combining advanced AI models for urban analysis:

  • Grounding DINO: Used for zero-shot segmentation of building features from street-level imagery.
  • GPT-4V: Provides detailed analyses, generating insights on architectural style, material types, and building conditions.
  • Data sources include publicly available repositories such as Google Street View and Mapillary, ensuring accessibility even in regions with limited data.

This methodology provides a scalable and automated approach to generate building-specific insights, forming the basis for a comprehensive resource cadastre that informs policy, economic planning, and sustainability efforts.

Case Studies

URBAN-AI was tested across six diverse cities to address specific urban challenges:

  • Zurich: Focus on historical conservation, with analysis of heritage value and architectural styles.
  • San Francisco: Assessment of seismic vulnerability in multi-story buildings.
  • Melbourne: Examination of energy efficiency in older building structures.
  • Mumbai: Analysis of urban morphology and high-density residential areas.
  • Cape Town: Identification of greening potential through green facades and roofs.
  • Rio de Janeiro: Evaluation of flood vulnerability in various architectural styles.

Results and Insights

The implementation of URBAN-AI demonstrates its effectiveness in generating detailed building material density maps and resource inventories. This data supports urban mining, circular construction initiatives, and policy development for sustainable cities.

Our results highlight the potential for AI-driven methodologies to enhance traditional urban planning approaches, providing cost-effective, scalable solutions for stakeholders worldwide.
